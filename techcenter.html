Community and Tech Center

4th Year Architecture Project

Mediums Used:

Revit Architecture, 3D Studio Max, Illustrator

The program requirements for this project were outlined as multi-use. Features were to include living spaces, rentable business locations, and a public community area. The location selected was a vacant former car dealership on the north side of the downtown main street of Royal Oak, MI. In laying out the space and several study models later, I concluded the best course of action was to create a 3-story structure. By placing community on the lowest level, I offered immediate access to the public. By placing the business one the second level, there was secondary access to both the public spaces and the private residence. The added benefit to this layout was ultimately that after business hours passed, the 2nd floor acted as a buffer between the public and private areas.

Having lived in apartments by this point in my life, I gained a certain insight as to what would make these living spaces desirable over others. Ample storage was more than a luxury, it was a necessity, as well as a patio so that tenants could enjoy a variety of outdoor activities such as gardening, grilling or simply taking in the sun and fresh air.
